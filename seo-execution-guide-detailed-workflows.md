## BEFORE YOU START ANYTHING: Run the Rank Map

**Purpose:** This diagnostic tells you EXACTLY what type of content to build. Never skip this.

1. Open **Lead Snap** (leadsnap.com — link in video description for 50% off first 3 months)
2. Add the client's business profile
3. Run a **rank map** for the primary target keyword (e.g., "plumber Houston")
4. Lead Snap will show rankings at **169 grid points** across the city:
   - **Green** = positions 1–3 (visible, getting calls)
   - **Yellow** = positions 4–10 (invisible but close)
   - **Red** = positions 11+ (Google doesn't associate business with this area/service)
5. In Lead Snap, note the **Top 3% metric** — the percentage of the total map area that is green
6. Run rank maps for the **top 3–4 competitor businesses** for the same keyword
7. Note their Top 3% scores (e.g., Gary, Indiana top player = 94%; Houston top player = 28.5%)
8. Export/save the CSV data from the rank map — you'll use this later for geographic content

**What the number tells you (use this throughout the process):**
- Your goal Top 3% = match the top players in your market
- Your **topical relevance threshold** = 25–50% of the top player's score
  - If top players are at 80% → your threshold is 30–40%
  - If top players are at 40% → your threshold is 15–20%
- **Below threshold** → build topical content (FAQs, supporting pages)
- **At or above threshold** → build geographical content (landmark pages)

## WORKFLOW A: GBP Optimization

### Step 1: Audit the GBP — Categories

1. Open **Google Chrome** (must be Chrome for the extension)
2. Install **GMB Everywhere** Chrome extension (search "GMB Everywhere Chrome extension" in the Chrome Web Store, click "Add to Chrome")
3. Go to **maps.google.com**
4. Search for a competitor in the same category (e.g., "plumber Houston")
5. Click on one of the top-ranked competitors in the results
6. In the business panel, click **"Find More"** (this button appears because GMB Everywhere is active)
7. A **category finder popup** will open showing:
   - **Top section:** All categories this specific business has chosen + how many GBPs nationally use each one (e.g., "Plumber — 1.5 million GBPs")
   - **Bottom section:** Related categories and their usage counts (e.g., "Drainage Service — 15,000 GBPs"; "Septic System Service — 60,000 GBPs")
8. Repeat steps 4–7 for 3–5 more top competitors to collect a wide list of related categories
9. Open **Claude** (claude.ai — preferred tool, not ChatGPT)
10. Use the **Category Selection Prompt** (grab from the School Community prompt library — link in video description):
    - Input: business type, city, list of services the business actually performs
    - Also paste in a full list of valid GBP categories (AI tends to make up fake ones — providing the list forces it to choose only real ones)
    - Output: recommended primary + up to 9 secondary categories
11. Go to the client's **Google Business Profile** (business.google.com)
12. Click **"Edit Profile"** → **"Business category"**
13. Set the **primary category** (most important — this is the main service)
14. Add **secondary categories** — minimum 2–4, up to 10 if all are relevant
    - Each category = another set of search terms Google may show you for

**Current state check:** Most businesses have 1 category. Getting to 4–6 puts you ahead of 90% of competitors.

---

### Step 2: Audit the GBP — Services

1. In the **Google Business Profile** dashboard, click **"Edit Profile"** → **"Services"**
2. Review what's currently listed (most businesses have 1–5 services)
3. Open **Claude**
4. Use the **Services Generation Prompt** (from the School Community prompt library):
   - Input: all categories you just added to the GBP
   - The prompt generates services that semantically match each category
   - Output: 20–40 services organized under the appropriate category
5. Back in GBP, for **each category**, add the relevant services:
   - Click the category name to expand it
   - Click **"Add a service"** and type the service name (it's a free-text box — type exactly what you want)
   - Repeat until each category has its relevant services populated
6. **Goal:** At minimum 20 services total; in competitive markets, aim for 30–40

**Organization rule:** Services must be assigned to the right category. "Water heater replacement" goes under "Plumber," not "Gas Installation Service." Google checks whether services semantically match their parent category.

---

### Step 3: Fill Out Every GBP Field

Go through each section in **business.google.com → Edit Profile** and fill out:

1. **Business Description**
   - Click **"Edit Profile"** → **"Description"**
   - Target **750 characters exactly** (the maximum Google allows)
   - Include: primary category + city, secondary categories, key services, what makes the business different
   - Write in Claude — include all of the above as inputs

2. **Photos**
   - Click **"Add photos"**
   - Upload at least **20 photos**:
     - Exterior photos (building/storefront/vehicle)
     - Interior photos (if applicable — office, shop)
     - Team photos (owner, staff)
     - Work in progress photos (jobs being done)
   - Take photos on smartphone — they will be **automatically geotagged** with location data

3. **Attributes**
   - Click **"Edit Profile"** → **"More"** → **"Attributes"**
   - Go through **every attribute** listed
   - Check **"Yes"** for every one that applies
   - Check **"No"** for every one that doesn't (don't leave blank — blank = unverified)
   - Examples: Veteran-owned, woman-owned, accepts credit cards, free estimates, ADA compliant, has bathroom, wheelchair accessible, etc.

4. **Products** (if applicable)
   - Add any products the business sells

5. **Posts** — handled in Step 4 (automation)

6. **Phone Number** — verify it matches exactly what will go on the website (character for character)

7. **Address** — verify it matches exactly what will go on the website (character for character)

8. **Website URL** — set this to the homepage URL (this is the "GBP landing page" Google crawls)

**Time check:** This entire audit — categories + services + filling every box — should take ~30 minutes.

7. **You're done with posts for the entire year** — set it and forget it

## WORKFLOW B: The Core 30 Website Build

### Architecture Overview

```
Homepage (GBP Landing Page)
├── Category Page 1 (e.g., Drainage Service Houston)
│   ├── Service Page 1a (e.g., Drain Cleaning Houston)
│   ├── Service Page 1b (e.g., Drain Repair Houston)
│   └── Service Page 1c (e.g., Sewer Line Inspection Houston)
├── Category Page 2 (e.g., Gas Installation Service Houston)
│   ├── Service Page 2a
│   └── Service Page 2b
└── Category Page 3 (e.g., Septic System Service Houston)
    ├── Service Page 3a
    └── Service Page 3b
```

**Total pages:** 1 homepage + 3–4 category pages + 25–30 service pages ≈ **30 pages**

Every page must:
- Have its target keyword in the title tag AND the H1
- Be linked editorially (in content, not nav/footer) from its parent page
- Get one external "not AI slop" link (see Workflow C)

---

### Step 1: Build the Homepage

The homepage = the GBP landing page (the URL entered in the "Website" field of the GBP).

**The 8 Consistency Signals (all required):**

1. **Title Tag:** `[Primary Category] [City] — [Unique Value Prop] | [Brand Name]`
   - Example: `Houston Plumber — Same Day Service | Acme Plumbing`
   - Must include: primary GBP category + city name

2. **H1 (Main Heading):** Must include primary category + city name
   - Example: `Houston's Most Trusted Plumber — Same Day Emergency Service`

3. **Google Maps Embed:**
   - Go to **maps.google.com**
   - Search for the business by name
   - Click the business → click **"Share"** → click **"Embed a map"**
   - Copy the `<iframe>` code
   - Paste the embed code into the homepage (in the website editor, switch to HTML/code view)

4. **Secondary Categories in H2s:**
   - Each secondary GBP category gets its own H2 subheading on the homepage
   - Under each H2, write 50–100 words: what the category is, why customers need it, what services fall under it
   - End each section with an **editorial link** to the dedicated category page (see linking instructions below)

5. **Review Widget:**
   - Install a Google Reviews widget on the homepage (e.g., via Elfsight, Google Review widget plugin, or your page builder's built-in option)
   - Displays live Google Business reviews on the homepage

6. **Address:** Must match GBP address **character for character** (same abbreviations, same format)

7. **Phone Number:** Must match GBP phone number **character for character**

8. **Local Business Schema:**
   - Add LocalBusiness JSON-LD schema to the homepage `<head>`
   - Include: name, address, phone, URL, geo coordinates, opening hours, same data as GBP

**How to write the homepage content:**

1. Open **Claude**
2. Use the **Core 30 Content Prompt** (from School Community prompt library):
   - Input fields:
     - Primary category (e.g., "Plumber")
     - City (e.g., "Houston")
     - Target keyword (same as primary category + city for homepage)
     - Target category (same as primary category for homepage)
     - Target length (aim for 800–1,200 words for homepage)
     - All secondary categories from the GBP
     - All services from the GBP
   - The prompt includes: writing instructions, list of words to avoid (AI tells), formatting requirements
3. Copy the generated content
4. **Human edit pass (required):**
   - Add real business personality and voice
   - Add images (client photos preferred, AI-generated acceptable)
   - Add callout boxes, tables where relevant
   - Ensure proper formatting: short paragraphs, headers, bullet points
   - Insert the Google Maps embed at a natural location
   - Add the review widget
   - Insert editorial links to category pages
5. Paste final content into website editor (WordPress, Webflow, Wix, etc.)

---

### Step 2: Build Category Pages (3–4 pages)

One category page per secondary GBP category.

**Target keyword format:** `[Category Name] [City]` — e.g., "Drainage Service Houston"

For each category page:

1. Open **Claude**
2. Use the **Core 30 Content Prompt** with these inputs:
   - Primary category: the parent category (e.g., "Plumber")
   - City: same city
   - Target keyword: `[This Category] [City]`
   - Target category: this specific category (e.g., "Drainage Service")
   - Target length: 600–900 words
   - List the services that fall under THIS specific category
3. **Page structure:**
   - **Title Tag:** `Drainage Service Houston | [Brand Name]`
   - **H1:** `Houston Drainage Service — [Value Prop]`
   - **Body intro:** 100–150 words about this category in Houston
   - **H2 for each service** under this category:
     - Write 50–100 words about the service
     - Include an **editorial link** to the dedicated service page
   - **CTA section** at the bottom (call now, get a quote, etc.)
4. Human edit pass (same as homepage — add images, formatting, personality)
5. Publish the page with the correct URL slug (e.g., `/drainage-service-houston/`)

---

### Step 3: Build Service Pages (25–30 pages)

One service page per service listed on the GBP.

**Target keyword format:** `[Service Name] [City]` — e.g., "Water Heater Replacement Houston"

For each service page:

1. Open **Claude**
2. Use the **Core 30 Content Prompt** with these inputs:
   - Primary category: the parent category
   - City: same city
   - Target keyword: `[This Service] [City]`
   - Target category: the parent category this service belongs to
   - Target length: 500–800 words
   - Secondary categories: list all GBP categories (for context)
   - Services: list all GBP services (for context)
3. **Page structure:**
   - **Title Tag:** `Water Heater Replacement Houston | [Brand Name]`
   - **H1:** `Houston Water Heater Replacement — [Value Prop]`
   - **Body:** Detailed, specific content about THIS service in THIS city:
     - Why customers in Houston need this service
     - What the service includes
     - How long it takes
     - What to expect during and after
     - Pricing range if applicable
     - NOT generic advice — specific to this service and city
   - **CTA section** at bottom
4. Human edit pass (add images, client photos, formatting, personality)
5. Publish with clean URL slug (e.g., `/water-heater-replacement-houston/`)

---

### Step 4: Internal Linking Structure

**The rule:** Use ONLY editorial links (links appearing inside paragraph content). Never rely on navigation links or footer links — Google sees those as structural and passes minimal authority.

**Linking map:**
- **Homepage** → links editorially to each **category page** (1 link per category, placed in the H2 paragraph for that category)
- **Category page** → links editorially to each **service page** under it (1 link per service, placed in the H2 paragraph for that service)
- **Service pages** → no outbound editorial links required (leaf nodes)

**How to write an editorial link:**
- Wrong: `"Learn more about drainage services here."`
- Right: `"For standing water or slow drains throughout the home, our [Houston drainage service](link) team can diagnose the root cause same day."`

The anchor text should include the target keyword of the destination page.

**When building supporting content pages later:**
- Core 30 pages → link to supporting FAQ/topical pages (inline, contextual)
- Supporting pages → link back to the relevant core 30 page
- Geographic pages → link to the relevant service page AND to a "Locations" hub page

## WORKFLOW C: Link Building

### Link Type 1: "Not AI Slop" Links (one per page, every page)

**Purpose:** External validation that tells Google "this content is worth reading." Without these, AI-generated content sits with zero authority and doesn't rank.

**What these are:** Medium-quality external links from reasonable, real websites. Not high-powered authority links. Just a digital paper trail proving the content exists and has value.

**Quantity needed:**
- Core 30 pages = 30 links (one per page)
- Each supporting topical content page = 1 additional link
- Each geographic content page = 1 additional link

**How to get them:**

**Option A: Source yourself**
1. Identify websites in adjacent niches (home improvement blogs, local news sites, industry publications)
2. Reach out via email: offer a guest post, quote contribution, or resource mention
3. Request they link to the specific page URL you need linked

**Option B: Use the fulfillment shop**
1. Go to **icecreamtruck.shop** (the creator's own link service)
2. Cost: ~$35 per link
3. Submit the page URL and they source a relevant link pointing to it
4. They handle sourcing, outreach, and placement

**Order:** Get these links placed as pages go live. Don't wait until all 30 pages are done — start linking page 1 when it publishes.

---

### Link Type 2: Chamber of Commerce Links

**Purpose:** Local authority signals that tell Google "this is a real, trusted business in this community." Google recognizes chambers as local authority sites — these are among the strongest local trust signals available.

**How to find them:**

1. Open **Google Maps** (maps.google.com)
2. Search `"chamber of commerce" [your city]`
3. Note all chambers within a **70-mile radius** of the business address
4. Check each chamber's website — confirm they have a **member directory** with links to member websites
5. Note the annual membership fee (typically $200–$300/year, often based on number of employees)

**How to join:**

1. Go to each chamber's website
2. Click **"Join"** or **"Become a Member"**
3. Fill out the membership application (business name, address, phone, website URL — use exact same info as GBP)
4. Pay the membership fee
5. After approval (usually 1–7 days), verify your business listing appears in their online directory with a live link to your website

**How many to join:** Join every chamber within 70 miles. Example: if there are 10 chambers nearby, join all 10. Each = a separate high-trust local link. Chambers don't care if you're 30 miles outside their primary city — if you're paying the membership fee, you're in the directory.

**Result:** Each chamber membership = one high-authority local link to your homepage (or the specific page URL they'll let you submit).

---

### Link Type 3: Local Sponsorship Links

**Purpose:** Additional local authority links from organizations that Google views as real community entities (youth sports leagues, charities, community events, local nonprofits).

**How to find them using AI:**

1. Open **Claude**
2. Use the **Local Sponsorship Finder Prompt** (from School Community prompt library):
   - Input: business type, city, budget range (optional)
   - The prompt crawls/synthesizes information about local organizations in the area that accept sponsors and have websites
   - Output: list of specific organizations, their websites, estimated sponsorship costs, and contact info
3. Visit each organization's website to verify:
   - They have an active website
   - They have a sponsors page or will add a link for sponsors
   - The sponsorship cost is reasonable

**How to secure the links:**

1. Contact the organization (email or phone — contact info on their website)
2. Ask about sponsorship packages — specifically ask if sponsors get a link on their website
3. Pay the sponsorship fee (typically $100–$500 for local youth sports; varies for events/charities)
4. Provide your exact business name and website URL as you want it listed
5. Confirm the link goes live on their website and check it

**Examples of qualifying organizations:**
- Youth baseball, soccer, football leagues
- Local charity galas and fundraisers
- Community events (festivals, fairs, runs/walks)
- Local nonprofits and foundations
- City-run contractor or vendor directories (e.g., "city's preferred contractor directory")

---

### Link Building Order

1. **Simultaneously with publishing core 30 pages:** Source and place one "not AI slop" link per page as each page goes live
2. **Week 1–2:** Join the primary city chamber of commerce (fastest to get live)
3. **Week 2–4:** Join all additional chambers within 70 miles
4. **Week 3–6:** Pursue local sponsorship links (these take longer to coordinate)
5. **Ongoing:** As you add topical and geographic supporting pages, source one link per new page

## WORKFLOW D: The Decision Framework

### Step 1: Run the Initial Rank Map

*(Already covered in the "Before You Start" section above — run this BEFORE any optimization.)*

1. Open **Lead Snap**
2. Run rank map for primary keyword (e.g., "plumber Houston")
3. Note the **baseline Top 3% score**
4. Run rank maps for top 3–4 competitors; note their Top 3% scores
5. Calculate your **topical relevance threshold** = 25–50% of the top player's Top 3% score

---

### Step 2: After Launching Core 30 — First Decision Point

**30 days after publishing the Core 30 pages and getting links:**

1. Open **Lead Snap**
2. Run a new rank map for the primary target keyword
3. Read the **Top 3% metric** that Lead Snap calculates automatically
4. Compare to your threshold:
   - **Below threshold** → go to Step 3A (topical content)
   - **At or above threshold** → go to Step 3B (geographic content)

---

### Step 3A: Build Topical Content (when below threshold)

**Goal:** Prove to Google that you are a genuine expert in this service category.

**Source 1: Google People Also Ask**

1. Open **Google.com** in Chrome
2. Search for the **service name only — NO city name** (e.g., "water heater replacement" not "water heater replacement Houston")
3. Scroll down the results page until you see the **"People also ask"** box (usually 4–5 questions)
4. Click on the first question to expand it — this triggers more questions to appear
5. Click on the new questions that appear — keep clicking to expand more
6. Continue until you have **20–30 relevant questions**
7. Copy all questions into a text file or Claude

**IMPORTANT:** Do NOT copy questions word for word. Google has been trained to recognize SEOs doing this. Instead, rephrase each question:
- Original: "How much does it cost to replace a water heater?"
- Rephrased: "What's the typical price for water heater replacement?"
Same meaning, different phrasing. Google's transformer model understands both — it won't penalize rephrasing, but it will recognize exact duplication patterns.

**Source 2: Reddit (via Claude)**

1. Open **Claude**
2. Use the **Reddit FAQ Finder Prompt** (from School Community prompt library):
   - Input: service type, city
   - Claude will search Reddit for real questions people in your area have asked about the service
   - Output: list of real, locally-specific questions (these are better than PAA — more specific, more local)
3. Add these to your question list

**How to use the FAQs:**

1. For each question, determine which core 30 page it belongs to:
   - General plumbing question → goes on homepage or main plumber category page
   - Water heater specific → goes on water heater service page
   - Drain specific → goes on drain service page

2. On the relevant core 30 page, add:
   - The rephrased question as a **subheading (H3)**
   - A **brief answer** of 30–50 words (enough to directly answer it)
   - An **editorial link** inline: e.g., "Water heater replacement typically costs $2,000–$4,000 in Houston depending on type and complexity. For a full breakdown, see our [complete guide to water heater replacement costs](link)." — that link points to the new supporting page

3. Create the **supporting content page** that the link points to:
   - Open Claude
   - Input: the specific question, service, city
   - Output: comprehensive answer — 400–800 words, detailed, specific, formatted
   - Title Tag: the rephrased question + city (e.g., "Water Heater Replacement Cost in Houston | [Brand]")
   - H1: same as title tag concept
   - Body: comprehensive answer with headers, specific price ranges, factors that affect cost, etc.
   - End with editorial link back to the main service page

4. Use the **FAQ Supporting Page Prompt** (from School Community prompt library):
   - Input: list of all questions you collected
   - Output: which questions should get their own full supporting page vs. just a brief answer on the existing page
   - Claude identifies which questions warrant deep-dive pages

5. Publish all supporting pages with clean URL slugs

6. **Get one "not AI slop" link per new supporting page**

7. **Keep building supporting content until your Top 3% hits your threshold**
   - Re-run rank map monthly
   - Check Top 3%
   - Still below threshold → build more supporting FAQs
   - Hit threshold → switch to geographic content

---

### Step 3B: Build Geographic Content (when at or above threshold)

**Goal:** Expand the radius from your address where Google will show you in the top 3.

**How to identify target areas:**

1. Open **Lead Snap** → open the current rank map
2. Look specifically for positions **4, 5, and 6** (yellow/orange dots — not red, not green)
3. These are areas where you're CLOSE to top 3 — a small push will get you there
4. Note the geographic area where each 4/5/6 dot is located

**How to identify landmarks for those areas:**

1. Open **Google Maps** (maps.google.com)
2. Zoom into the area where you have a 4/5/6 ranking
3. Look for named landmarks Google already recognizes:
   - **Neighborhood names** (e.g., "Harvest Bend," "Memorial Heights")
   - **Park names** (e.g., "Memorial Park")
   - **Subdivision names** (e.g., "Wagon Wheel Estates")
   - **Major intersections** (e.g., "near the intersection of Windfield Road and Fallbrook Drive")
   - **Shopping centers** with names
   - **Schools** with names
   - **Lakes or bodies of water** with names
4. **Only use landmarks that appear on Google Maps** — if Google has named it, Google considers it relevant geography
5. Target areas where **real people live and work** (residential neighborhoods, commercial areas) — not industrial zones or unpopulated areas

**Target keyword format:** `[Service] [Landmark Name] [City]`
- Examples:
  - "Plumber Maple Leaf Gardens Houston"
  - "Plumber Wagon Wheel Estates Houston"
  - "Plumber near Memorial Park Houston"
  - "Plumber Harvest Bend Houston"
  - "Plumber Ranchstone Houston"

**How to write geographic content:**

1. Open **Claude**
2. Use the **Geographic Content Prompt** (from School Community prompt library):
   - Input: service, landmark name, city, (optional) rank map CSV data with demographic info
   - For demographic prioritization: paste the CSV export from Lead Snap into Claude alongside the geographic content prompt
3. The prompt instructs Claude to write content that is **genuinely local**, not just keyword stuffing:
   - Mention the specific neighborhood/area and what it's known for
   - Reference common service issues in that area (e.g., older homes = cast iron pipes; newer construction = PEX plumbing)
   - Reference nearby local businesses or landmarks
   - Include the **driving route your technicians take** to get to that neighborhood
   - Include the **driving route customers would take** to visit your location (if applicable)
4. **Page structure:**
   - **Title Tag:** `Plumber in Harvest Bend Houston | [Brand Name]`
   - **H1:** `Harvest Bend's Trusted Houston Plumber`
   - **Body:** 400–600 words of genuinely local content (as described above)
   - **Editorial link** back to the main service page (e.g., "For water heater service in Harvest Bend, see our Houston water heater replacement page")
5. Publish with clean URL slug (e.g., `/plumber-harvest-bend-houston/`)
6. **Get one "not AI slop" link per geographic page**

**Create a Locations Hub Page:**

1. Create a new page on the site titled something like "Areas We Serve" or "Houston Service Locations"
2. List every geographic page with editorial links to each one
3. Link to this hub page from the homepage (editorial or navigation)
4. This hub page acts as the parent in the internal linking hierarchy for all geographic pages

**Internal linking for geographic pages:**
- Geographic page → links to the relevant service page (e.g., Harvest Bend Plumber page → links to Water Heater Replacement Houston page)
- Geographic page → links to the Locations hub page
- Locations hub → links to all geographic pages
- Core service pages → can optionally link to geographic pages in a "service areas" section

**Keep targeting positions 4–6:**
- As authority grows, you'll start appearing in new areas at positions 4–6
- Re-run rank map every 2 weeks when building geographic content
- Each time you see new 4/5/6 dots, identify the landmark, write the page, get the link

---

### Step 4: The Ongoing Monthly Loop

**Every month, repeat this cycle:**

1. Open **Lead Snap**
2. Run a new rank map for your primary keyword (and secondary keywords as business grows)
3. Note the **Top 3% score**
4. Compare to threshold:
   - **Below threshold** → write 3–5 more topical FAQ supporting pages + get links
   - **At or above threshold, gaps in coverage** → write 3–5 more geographic landmark pages + get links
   - **Dominating everywhere (high Top 3%)** → maintain mode (see below)

**Maintain and defend mode (when you're dominating):**
- Keep GBP automated posts running (Lead Snap handles this — nothing to do)
- Keep automated review responses running
- Keep weekly geotagged photo uploads running
- Respond to any reviews that need attention
- Add new services to GBP as the business evolves (each new service = new opportunity for a service page)
- Monitor competitor rank maps — if a competitor starts gaining ground, identify where and target those areas
- Consider adding a second GBP location if business expands → repeat entire system from scratch for the new location (new GBP → new Core 30 → new links → new supporting/geographic content)

**Scaling to a second location:**
1. Set up new GBP for new location (repeat Workflow A)
2. Build new Core 30 pages specific to the new location's city (repeat Workflow B)
3. Get links for new pages (repeat Workflow C)
4. Run rank maps for new location, apply decision framework (repeat Workflow D)
5. The fundamentals don't change — the system is identical, just targeted at a new city
